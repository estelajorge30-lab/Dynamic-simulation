Abstract

Texto para copiar/pegar en el manuscrito.

The Challenge: High-fidelity simulation offers experiential training for time-critical conditions, yet proprietary systems remain inaccessible for many institutions in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). Although AI-assisted "vibe coding" has enabled educators to construct static applications, its capacity to generate real-time, interactive physiological simulations remains underexplored.

The Solution: We describe a clinician-led workflow using large language models to create The Dynamic Simulation Framework, an open-source architecture capable of visualizing physiological signals and embedding user actions into real-time changes.

Implementation: Using natural-language prompts, we built three prototypes: (1) an Arrhythmia Trainer; (2) a Multi-Specialty Signal Suite (EEG, Spirometry, Obstetrics); and (3) a Gamified Defibrillator Simulator with critical care intervention logic. All implementations were developed with cloud-based AI tools (Gemini 3.0 pro in build mode) and deployed online without professional engineering support.

Lessons Learned: This proof-of-concept suggests that AI-assisted development offers a feasible pathway for institutions to prototype responsive, visually realistic simulations without specialized programming skills. Future studies should evaluate educational outcomes and integration into curricular settings.
